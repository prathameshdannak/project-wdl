Tourist Safety Tips

Important safety advice for tourists visiting India, based on guidelines from the Ministry of Tourism and state tourism departments.

📌 General Safety Tips

🪪
Always carry your Tourist ID Keep your SafeYatra Tourist ID with you at all times. It helps police identify and assist you quickly during an emergency.
📞
Save emergency numbers Save 100 (Police), 108 (Ambulance), 1363 (Tourist Helpline) in your phone before you travel.
👜
Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas Keep your wallet in your front pocket at busy markets, bus stands, and railway stations. Use a money belt for valuables.
🌅
Avoid isolated areas after dark Stick to well-lit, populated areas at night. Avoid unfamiliar lanes and beaches after sunset without company.
🚕
Use only licensed transport Use government-approved taxis, auto-rickshaws with meter, or registered apps (Ola, Uber). Avoid accepting rides from unknown strangers.
💊
Carry a basic first-aid kit Especially important for adventure tourism areas (Rishikesh, Leh, Manali). Include ORS packets, antiseptic, and any personal medicines.
📍
Share your itinerary with someone Always inform a family member or hotel staff about your daily travel plan, especially if visiting remote locations.
💧
Drink only packaged or boiled water Avoid tap water in unfamiliar places. Buy sealed packaged water from reputed brands to prevent water-borne illness.

👩 Safety Tips for Women Tourists

👮‍♀️
Know the Women Helpline number: 1091 Dial 1091 (Women Helpline) or 112 (Emergency) immediately if you feel unsafe. Most states have dedicated women tourist police.
🏨
Stay in government-approved hotels Choose hotels listed on the Ministry of Tourism website. Inform the front desk of your solo travel status.
🧣
Dress modestly at religious sites Cover shoulders and legs when visiting temples, mosques, or churches. Carry a scarf or dupatta to avoid unwanted attention.

ℹ️ About This System

SafeYatra is a proposed Smart Tourist Safety & Incident Response System designed as an engineering project.

Proposed integrations (for future development):

  • Integration with Aadhaar / DigiLocker for tourist identity verification
  • Real-time CCTV feed from ASI (Archaeological Survey of India) monument cameras
  • Live data feed from NCRB crime database
  • API connection with State Police Control Rooms
  • GPS tracking via tourist's mobile number using DoT TRAI APIs
  • Integration with National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) for disaster alerts
